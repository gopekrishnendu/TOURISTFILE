VICTORIA MEMORIAL


About Victorla Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a famous monument located in Kolkata (formerly Calcutta), West Bengal, India. It was built in memory of Queen Victoria and is now a museum and tourist attraction.

The construction of the Victoria Memorial started in 1906 and was completed in 1921. It was designed by British architect William Emerson and is made of white marble. The memorial has a large dome, which is about 184 feet high, and is surrounded by gardens and water bodies.

Inside the Victoria Memorial, there are various galleries that display historical artifacts, paintings, and sculptures from the British colonial era in India. Some of the most notable exhibits include a painting of Queen Victoria, a collection of rare books and manuscripts, and an exhibition of portraits and paintings of the Nawabs of Bengal.
