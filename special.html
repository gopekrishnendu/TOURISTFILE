Dakkhineshwar Kali Temple

About Dakkhineshwari Kali Temple

Dakshineswar Kali Temple is a Hindu temple located in Dakshineswar, Kolkata, India. The temple is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Kali, who is worshipped in the form of Bhavatarini. The temple was built by Rani Rashmoni, a philanthropist and devotee of Kali, in 1855.

The temple complex is spread over a large area and consists of twelve shrines dedicated to various gods and goddesses, including Radha-Krishna, Rama-Sita, and Hanuman. The main temple is a nine-spired structure with a central shrine that houses the idol of Kali.

The temple is known for its association with Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, a 19th-century mystic and saint who served as the temple's head priest. Ramakrishna's teachings emphasized the universality of all religions and the importance of direct experience of God.
